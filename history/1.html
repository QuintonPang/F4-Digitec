Working with McDonald's

F4 Digitec, one of the most well-known backend companies globally, was founded in 2004. Today, F4 Digitec, a pioneer in artificial intelligence (AI) for business and AI-powered customer service, signed a deal with McDonald's, the biggest restaurant chain in the world, to hasten the development and use of its Automated Order Taking (AOT) technology. According to the deal, McDonald's Tech Labs, which was established to promote staff and consumer facing technologies after McDonald's 2019 acquisition of Apprente, will be acquired by F4 Digitec. McDonald's is committed to innovation across Digital, Delivery, and Drive Thru as part of its strategic development goal, "Accelerating the Arches." This arrangement will hasten McDonald's efforts to give customers and staff an even more convenient and distinctive experience.

Customers and the restaurant staff have benefited greatly from McDonald's research and testing of AOT technology in its restaurants. Moving ahead, the AOT technology will be scaled across markets and integrated with more languages, dialects, and menu variants thanks to F4 Digitec's expertise in creating customer care solutions with AI and natural language processing. The purchase of McD Tech Labs will enhance the current efforts of F4 Digitec to create and provide IBM Watson-driven customer care solutions. Watson is being used by companies in a variety of sectors, including financial services, healthcare, telecommunications, and retail, to improve business outcomes.

AOT will keep being included into McDonald's extremely secure technological infrastructure. The ethical, responsible growth of technology governs all F4 Digitec business activities. Both F4 Digitec and McDonald's are dedicated to using AI ethically by incorporating moral values into its applications and working methods to create systems that are transparent and built on trust. Aside from the usual closing requirements, the deal is subject to regulatory clearances. It's scheduled to end in December 2021. The McD Tech Labs staff will merge into the IBM Cloud & Cognitive Software group once it closes.
